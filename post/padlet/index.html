Padlet

Overview

Padlet is collaboration tool for collecting articles, videos, music, text and photos on a online virtual bulletin board. Padlets allow for multiple users to simultaneously add, read and organize matters usually based on a central theme or idea. The materials can be organized in different formats though the most common seems to use columns to further break down the overall idea into subtopics.

Homepage: padlet.com Platform: MacOS or Online (accessed via browser)

Cost

Padlet has a free trial which allow for one Padlet. For schools they offer a plan at $2000USD/yr for up to 500 teacher and students.

Advantages and Challenges

Advantages

  • Easy to setup and provide access.
  • Works both synchronously and asynchronously.
  • Allows students and teacher to visually separate content.

Challenges

  • Cost.
  • Requires students to be motivated to participate.
  • Content needs to be monitored for appropriateness.
  • Access to site needs to be made easy for students.

Learning Strategy

From (Walsh, 2018), Padlet would be used as part of the peer instruction learning technique. Padlet allows students to participate in the gathering of material to be used in their education. Brainstorming ideas for a report, gathering reference and organized materials, or to allowing students to find resources to teach each other class materials are just some of the ways it could be used to meet this goal.

Pedagogical function

Padlet would fall into the following categories of pedagogical function: organizational technologies, communication and collaboration technologies, presentation technologies. The multi-column design allows users to separate materials however they wish, choosing division that make sense to them. The tool allows for all students to participate in the gathering and organization of materials to fit their theme. Finally, the visual nature of the tool allows students to choose how to present their material.

Accessibility Concerns

The University of Northampton has a website that outlines the accessibility concerns for Padlet. The main concerns are for students with visual challenges. The site does not allow for the tagging of images with alternate text for screen readers, nor does it have a low vision colour contrast.

Special Equipment Required

No special equipment required.
